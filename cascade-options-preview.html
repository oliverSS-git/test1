A living brief. Click any highlighted field to edit. Upstream doctrine, tasks, and gates are configured in Sections 01 and 02; this page configures environments, the content pack, and — derived from all of the above — the hardware.
3a · Environments & Conditions

Training is configured to run in Day/Night · Open/Urban environments, under Day · Civil twilight · Night (unaided + NVG) lighting conditions, with Clear · Rain · Fog · Dust weather variants that stress detection cues.

The geographies in scope are CONUS installation perimeter · OCONUS austere · Flight line · ECP / gate · IBD sector — these are the terrains the scoring rubric has to hold up under.

3b · Content Pack

The configured content pack is ICST C-UAS Pack v1, a bundle of scenarios, NPC profiles, environmental variants, and scoring rubrics mapped to the upstream doctrine, tasks, environments, and gates.

The pack ships with a six-stage progression × day/night × single-target and swarm variants. The NPC library includes Group 1 & Group 2 sUAS behaviors, VACR-profiled targets, and civilians / OCP-uniformed friendlies for the ROE gate.

3c · Hardware Loadout — Derived

Given the upstream configuration, the readiness sensors in scope are Mossberg 590, Remington 870, and Benelli M4 replicas — the shotgun variants that match configured Defender load-outs.

The platform class is Standalone VR — on-HMD compute, no tethered workstation, no base-network dependency, because that class of kit is the one that holds up under austere IT, variable bandwidth, and fast fielding — the conditions implied by the upstream configuration.

Kit quantities (per-installation kit · qty driven by squadron count × airmen per flight) are sized in Section 04 where deployment-scale assumptions live.

The layering moment. When a second capability — say M18 pistol sustainment — is added later, the upstream cascade re-runs: new doctrine, new tasks, new environments, new gates, new pack. The hardware doesn't. Same headset, same compute, same kit. New capability, same boxes.
