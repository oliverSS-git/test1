C-UAS TRAINING
READINESS: UNKNOWN

As of today, AFSFC cannot verify C-UAS readiness across the force. Not because the data doesn't exist — because it was never designed to flow.

Squadrons generate training records inside vendor-specific platforms. MAJCOMs receive interpreted reports. AFSFC receives summaries of those summaries. Each layer compresses signal into noise. No common schema exists across vendors. No automated aggregation. No enterprise-level view of readiness that reflects what's actually happening on the range today.

No Deployment Rails

Field-Level Gap

C-UAS Training vs. Skeet Range

Installations without dedicated C-UAS platforms default to existing ranges. Airmen train on skeet — a proxy for a threat environment that no longer matches the operational reality. No scoring against doctrine. No comparison to AFSFC-defined proficiency standards.

MAJCOM Accumulation

Requirements Without Rails

When AFSFC issues new C-UAS requirements, they land at the MAJCOM layer. MAJCOMs interpret. Squadrons receive MAJCOM-interpreted versions. No automated deployment mechanism ensures the original standard reaches every unit intact.

Commander Visibility

No Real-Time Readiness Picture

Squadron commanders cannot see live proficiency data. MAJCOM commanders work from stale reports. AFSFC cannot verify whether the force is ready for a C-UAS contingency on any given day.

Structural Result

Two Broken Pipes

Nothing flows up automatically. Nothing deploys down reliably. The result isn't a training gap that can be solved by adding hours — it's a structural gap that requires a new operating model.
