Finding 01

Fragmented at the Edge

The field was never given a common substrate to build on.

At the squadron level, the result is a patchwork of incompatible systems — vendor-proprietary platforms, legacy software, manual spreadsheets, and paper logs — none sharing a common data format.

Key Gap
No common standard exists. No C-UAS training pathway is in place at the squadron level.
Implication
Multiple vendors. Inconsistent formats. No signal can travel between levels of the hierarchy.

No Data Flows Up

Readiness is assumed — because it cannot be measured.

Incompatible systems and no common schema. Data generated at the squadron level cannot be aggregated — there is no shared format for readiness reporting.
Why Data Can't Flow Up — Reason 2
Commanders layer more training requirements because no authoritative signal exists. Without data, the default response is more requirements — compounding the gap.
