Structural Gap Analysis
PRG-REF: FA8003-26-SSVR  |  PROGRAM REVIEW — SLIDE 3 OF 5
The Two Broken Pipes — Why This Was Built
Diagnosis of the structural conditions that necessitated this program.
Upward Pipe
STATUS: DEGRADED

Readiness data could not aggregate upward from squadron to MAJCOM or AFSFC. Incompatible vendor systems, no common schema, no standardized output format.

Consequence: AFSFC operated on assumed readiness, not measured readiness. Commanders layered additional training requirements because no authoritative signal existed.

  • 7 distinct vendor systems at squadron
  • No common data format
  • No automated reporting
  • Manual AAR process only
Downward Pipe
STATUS: ABSENT

When AFSFC issued updated requirements (C-UAS, AFFORGEN), no deployment pathway existed. Requirements accumulated in layers at MAJCOM without reaching squadron as updated training content.

Consequence: Institutional accumulation. MAJCOMs stacked requirements. Squadrons received conflicting or duplicative guidance. No single authoritative standard enforced.

  • No content deployment rails
  • MAJCOM interpretation layers
  • C-UAS requirement unaddressed
  • Assumed ready — no data confirming
Two broken pipes. Nothing flows up. Nothing deploys down. The result isn't a training gap — it's a structural one. The drone is already in the airspace. The airman is still on the skeet range.
