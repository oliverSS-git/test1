1bAdjacent Mandates▾
What Else Touches These Same Airmen?
Adjacent mandates that apply to the same personnel — same platform, same evidence pipeline.
CATM Qualification — Small Arms & Crew-Served
AFI 36-2654 ▾
CATM is building the live kinetic C-UAS range. The virtual side teaches the fight — scenarios, mixed engagement, judgment. M18, M4A1 qual tables are already deployed at Navy CNIC and available for AF. M320 sustainment is an identified near-term gap.
Ready Airman Training (RAT)
DAFMAN 36-2655 ▾
RAT task-code alignment is already being pursued through HAF/A3T. V-CSTR modules are mapped to RAT-relevant tasks. Training reps on ICST generate evidence that can feed RAT readiness gates — same session, no separate event.
Deployment Familiarization — Perishable Skills
AFFORGEN ▾
AFSFC leadership has identified the core gap: Airmen don’t touch deployment-relevant equipment until they arrive downrange. Radios, tactical comms, LOGDET equipment, land nav, buddy care. The platform provides home-station reps on low-density, perishable skills — repeatable proficiency beyond initial familiarization.
Unit Readiness Reporting
AFI 10-201 ▾
AFI 10-201 requires measurable readiness — but most C-UAS training today proves attendance, not capability. The Evidence Engine produces structured session records mapped to standards. Output is a readiness proof artifact commanders can defend when asked.
VTS Validation — Institutional Acceptance Pathway
AFSFC / CADMS ▾
AFSFC is the requirements owner and VTS validation authority via CADMS. AETC/A3X owns the credentialing and accreditation pathway. The objective isn’t just technical performance — it’s institutional acceptance. Training that “counts” flows through these gates.
Use-of-Force / Non-Lethal Weapons
AFI 31-118 ▾
SF units with LE missions rarely get consistent force-continuum reps between NLW certification cycles. Baton, OC, CEW measurement architecture fielded across Ontario law enforcement services — same structural approach applicable to home-station SF missions. No separate procurement.
M320 Grenade Launcher — Sustainment & Procedural Proficiency
AFSFC Priority ▾
M320 is in the AF weapon family and explicitly included in the simulator requirement. Already identified as an AFSFC near-term priority alongside C-UAS. The requirement is repeatable procedural proficiency beyond initial familiarization. Same platform, same evidence pipeline.
Tactical Communications — Radio & SALUTE Reporting
Deployment Readiness ▾
Surfaced repeatedly in AFSFC calls as a priority between C-UAS and M320. Radio procedures, SALUTE reporting, dispatch coordination — all scored within the same scenario environment. Comms proficiency is already a measured behavior in the C-UAS kill chain; extending it to standalone comms training is configuration, not new development.

Configuration Baseline Co-authored · Draft
The ICST C-UAS training requirements are derived from AFI 31-101 — Integrated Defense, DAFI 31-131 Vol 2, AFJQS SEI 3CS, SFTRG C-sUAS TTPs.

This team has agreed that every scored behavior will trace directly to these governing documents. The training system maintains a requirements traceability matrix linking each evaluated behavior to its authorizing directive. When source directives are updated, evaluation criteria can be reconfigured within 30 days of validated change notification — without requiring instructor re-certification. Formal POI updates through AETC channels proceed on their standard timeline.

 This requirements trace assumes access to current source directives. Sections 01–04 remain in DRAFT pending validation against authenticated versions of the governing documents listed above.
