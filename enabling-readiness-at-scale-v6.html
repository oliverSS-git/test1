Current-State Gaps

Training by Waiver
Across domains — crowd control, non-lethal deployment, C-UAS — commanders signed training waivers by the stack. No accredited protocol. Documentation to satisfy compliance, not to prove capability.
One Brief, No Reps
Whether it was a Dronebuster, a use-of-force scenario, or a crowd situation, the average Defender saw training once — usually a slide deck before deployment — and never touched it again.
No Domestic Operations Framework
Security Forces manage base access, crowd control, and non-lethal force situations at home station. No structured, repeatable training existed for the non-kinetic side of the mission.
Point Solutions, Not a System
Units that did have training bought it piece by piece. No common measurement. No shared data. No way to demonstrate readiness across the full spectrum.
